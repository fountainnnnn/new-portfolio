What is SP70?

SP70 refers to the 70th anniversary celebration of Singapore Polytechnic (SP). In 2024, Singapore Polytechnic will mark its 70th year since its establishment, started as a modest five-storey building on Prince Edward Road has expanded into Singapore's largest polytechnic—a vast campus covering 38 hectares of lush greenery and home to over 230,000 distinguished alumni.

For All Ages

As a way to commemorate and celebrate Singapore Polytechnic's 70th anniversary, they have launched a book titled 'For All Ages, Singapore Polytechnic At Seventy', recognizing some of the remarkable individuals and moments that made the current SP.

Personal/Work Life

Personal and professional lives are experiencing significant transformations. A crucial element of SP's "future-ready" identity is maintaining global connectivity. This involves cultivating a curiosity about the diverse aspects of the world.

This global perspective also fosters empathy towards people from different cultures, which is essential in many professions. Additionally, it enhances the ability to comprehend the needs of users, consumers, markets, and audiences.

Industry Disruptions

The entire industrial landscape has become significantly more disruptive due to groundbreaking advancements in fields like artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, the Internet of Things (IoT), and robotics.

To be prepared for the future, SP must embrace and adapt to changes in the industry, whether these come from technological advancements or other workplace shifts. Foreseeing future developments is the only way to protect against the most severe disruptions and displacements.

Global Shifts

Among all the global challenges, climate change is considered the most critical by many people. At Singapore Polytechnic, this issue is also of great importance. Efforts to prepare students for the future emphasize addressing sustainability, always considering a comprehensive, big-picture approach.

This commitment is most evident in the Polytechnic's Sustainable Innovation Project (SIP), one of the 10 modules in the Common Core Curriculum. This module provides a learning experience that culminates in a capstone project, which students undertake in their second year of study.

SP Timeline

1951

Local businessmen met at Adelphi Hotel's roof garden to discuss idea of a polytechnic

1958

Completion of Prince Edward Road campus with an enrolment of 2,800 students registered for 58 courses to prepare them for external examinations

1959

Official opening of Singapore Polytechnic at Prince Edward Road by Prince Philip, the Duke of Edinburgh on 24 February 1959.

1965

Professional diploma courses gradually replaced by degree courses

1979

Dover Road campus officially opened by Prime Minister Lee Kuan Yew on 7 July. The ceremony was held at the Quadrangle between the Admin Building and the Library.

1994

SP became the first poly to introduce the Diploma in Optometry.
Official opening of Fourth Phase campus development project by Minister for Education Lee Yock Suan.

2004

Singapore Polytechnic turned 50. His Excellency, Mr S R Nathan, President of the Republic of Singapore (second from right), launched the yearlong celebrations on 13 February at the official opening of the new Convention Centre and Plaza

2005

Singapore Polytechnic (SP) buried a time capsule to signify the close of the year long celebrations and the opening of a new chapter in the SP story.

2014

SP celebrates its 60th anniversary.
SP's new vision - A caring community of inspired learners committed to serve with mastery was announced.

2024

SP celebrates its 70th anniversary!
